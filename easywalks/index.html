ABOUT US

Our hiking in Scotland group tours accommodate between 4 and 12 guests. We hike from 4 to 9 miles per day and include optional walks wherever possible. The hiking on our tours ranges from gentle walking on pristine white sand beaches in the Outer Hebrides to challenging hiking in the Isle of Skye. All of our tours are accompanied by our expert guides who provide comprehensive insights into the areas visited.

At Hike Scotland we specialise in providing the perfect backdrop for walkers of all abilities to immerse themselves in the unique culture that makes Scotland such a special place. Hiking in Scotland with us allows our guests to learn, on the trail, about our country's colourful and sometimes tragic history, our cornucopia of wildlife and flora, incredible geology and of course unrivalled stunning scenery.

We take great care in putting together wild walks and luxurious nights. Our expert guides lead you on a discovery of some of Scotland's most remote corners, exploring the Highlands, islands, glens and bens.

GUIDED TOURS

We offer three distinct and different tours suitable for people of all skill level.

Glen Coe

This popular tour takes you to one of the most spectacular and beautiful places in Scotland. he glen, approaching from the east on the main A82 road, is surrounded by wild and precipitous mountains. Further west at Invercoe, the landscape has a softer beauty before the main entrance to the glen.

Isle of Skye

Put simply, the mould was broken after the Isle of Skye was created, leaving an island that is unique in its spectacular mountain landscapes and superb coastal scenery. This carefully crafted itinerary affords an insight into these two contrasting aspects of the ' Winged Isle ' and illustrates the enormous diversity that Skye has to offer.

Loch Lomond

This journey through the Western Highlands follows age old drovers' trails and ancient military roads, from the stunning bonnie banks of Loch Lomond in Scotland's Southern Uplands to Rannoch Moor, one of the most desolate and windswept places in the British Isles.
